The Song of Rosana and the Evil King

Introduction

Rosana is a village girl born from a handsome sailor called Tarma and a beautiful performer called Jirmak. She was born in a village located in a dry land called Hikmid. Hikmid was two miles far from the sea and had been colonized by a very powerful, rich empire called Boparl. The people of Hikmid were treated like war slaves, yet they were allowed a strange kind of freedom: the Hikmidians were allowed to do whatever they wanted as long as they respected the law.

Boparl consisted of seven states: Kahli, Gorma, Tunera, Farisa, Hjortigar, Hakma, and the capital region. These six states were governed by the king's most trusted ministers, while the king, Shirkisir, his wife Kumata, and his three concubines lived in the capital, Tumar. Tumar was located in the center of the empire, a beautiful city where money flowed, businesses thrived, and gold miners searched for gold. Hikmid was within the state of Farisa to the south of the capital. Rosana's father, Tarma, was a righteous man, the son of a fisherman, Irsha, and a housekeeper named Luisa.

1

Introduction: Jirmak's Past

Rosana's mother, Jirmak, had a different beginning. She was abandoned by her parents at the doorstep of a pleasure house. She was found by Kapoor, the prostitute building owner. Kapoor, charmed by Jirmak's beauty, adopted her and raised her as her daughter. Jirmak was sent to school and trained in the art of dance and music. Kapoor raised Jirmak to carry herself with grace in high social status, harboring a secret hope that Jirmak would marry a rich man and move to a mansion in the capital with servants, enabling Jirmak to provide for Kapoor in her old age.

As Jirmak grew, her beauty became legendary. Men and women from all over the village would gather just to catch a glimpse of her dancing and singing. Jirmak grew more beautiful and elegant day by day. Though many suitors proposed, Kapoor rejected them all, having big plans for her daughter's future[span_0](start_span)[span_0](end_span).

The Rescue and the Vow

One day early in the morning, just as the sun began to break, Jirmak attempted a desperate escape. Driven by the weight of her mother's obsession, she took a small boat to the sea, hoping to find a life of her own. While trying to sail in her little boat, a storm started, and the waves were unforgiving. Jirmak slipped and fell into the sea and she did not know how to swim[span_1](start_span)[span_1](end_span).

Luckily, Tarma was returning from a long night of fishing with his father, Irsha. Seeing the struggle, Tarma dove deep into the water to save Jirmak. When he brought her back to the sand, Jirmak was not breathing. He tried to revive her, but she remained still. In a final desperate act, Tarma breathed his own air into her lungs. It worked, and she opened her eyes and screamed. Jirmak looked into the eyes of her rescuer, and they both fell deeply in love[span_2](start_span)[span_2](end_span).

Secret Marriage and Exile

Jirmak started meeting Tarma in secret, and their love became deeper and deeper. Two years later, Tarma proposed to Jirmak. Jirmak joyfully accepted, but their happiness was met with a wall of gold and pride. Luisa, Tarma’s mother, could not accept Jirmak's upbringing. She was greatly displeased and banned her son Tarma from seeing Jirmak. Kapoor, Jirmak’s mother, in her greed for wealth, banned Jirmak from seeing Tarma or going outside alone[span_3](start_span)[span_3](end_span).

Faced with a world that wanted to keep them apart, Tarma and Jirmak chose each other. They married in secret, a quiet vow between their two souls. When the truth came out, they were both cast out by the villagers and disowned by their families. Jirmak's mother found out and decided to disown her; Luisa was displeased by her son's decision and decided to kick him out. With nothing but love and small savings, they built a small hut outside the village[span_4](start_span)[span_4](end_span).
