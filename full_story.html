The Song of Rosana and the Evil King

"In the sacred halls of memory and empire, the book awaits its readers..."

1

Introduction: The Origins

Rosana is a village girl born from a handsome sailor called Tarma and a beautiful performer called Jirmak. She was born in a village located in a dry land called Hikmid, two miles far from the sea. Hikmid had been colonized by a very powerful rich empire called Boparl, where people were treated like war slaves yet allowed a strange kind of freedom: they could do whatever they wanted as long as they respected the law.

Boparl consisted of seven states: Kahli, Gorma, Tunera, Farisa, Hjortigar, Hakma, and the capital region. Governed by trusted ministers, the capital Tumar was a beautiful city where money flowed, businesses thrived, and gold miners searched for gold. Hikmid was within the state of Farisa to the south of the capital. Rosana's father Tarma was a righteous man, the son of a fisherman Irsha and housekeeper Luisa.

2

Introduction: Jirmak's Beginnings

Rosana's mother, Jirmak, had a different beginning. Abandoned at the doorstep of a pleasure house, she was found by Kapoor, the prostitute building owner. Charmed by Jirmak's beauty, Kapoor adopted and raised her as a daughter, sending her to school and training her in the art of dance and music with the hope that Jirmak would marry a rich man and move to a mansion in the capital.

As Jirmak grew, her beauty became legendary, drawing crowds just to catch a glimpse of her. Though many suitors proposed, Kapoor rejected them all, harboring big plans for her daughter's future.

3

The Escape & Rescue

One day early in the morning, driven by the weight of her mother's obsession, Jirmak attempted a desperate escape on a small boat into the sea. A sudden storm started, and the unforgiving waves caused Jirmak to slip and fall into the sea, unable to swim.

Luckily, Tarma returning from a long night of fishing with his father Irsha saw the struggle and dove deep to save Jirmak. When brought back to the sand, she wasn't breathing. In a final desperate act, Tarma breathed his own air into her lungs. It worked, and she opened her eyes, locking starlit gazes with her rescuer as they fell deeply in love.

4

Secret Vows & Exile

Two years later, Tarma proposed. Jirmak joyfully accepted, but their happiness met a wall of gold and pride. Luisa, Tarma’s mother, disapproved of Jirmak's upbringing and banned Tarma from seeing her, while Kapoor banned Jirmak from seeing Tarma in her greed for wealth.

Facing a world that wanted to keep them apart, they married in secret. When discovered, they were cast out by villagers and disowned by their families. With nothing but love and small savings, they built a small hut outside the village.

5

The Beginning: The Birth of Rosana

To provide, Tarma became a sailor navigating waves to trade merchandise in the capital. Jirmak stayed behind on solid ground, her near-drowning leaving her with a deep fear of the water. Instead, she leaned into her gifts as a renowned singer and dancer at a famous theater in a neighboring village.

Yet despite their stable life, their families kept their distance. One year later, Jirmak was approached in the bustling market by a powerful prophet named Kafura[span_0](start_span)[span_0](end_span).

6

The Meeting of the Prophet

Kafura looked deep into Jirmak's eyes and delivered a chilling revelation: "You will give birth to the most beautiful woman in the entire world. But greatness comes with a price. Your daughter will face trials of immense pain... yet she will rise as a powerful queen destined to deliver your people from the grip of the colonizers.[span_1](start_span)"[span_1](end_span)

Jirmak cried out in terror, begging to know how to save her child from suffering. Kafura replied that her path was written like Esther or Ruth, but he could help delay the prophecy, vowing to return the moment the child was born[span_2](start_span)[span_2](end_span).

7

The Cost of the Prophecy

Jirmak ran home to share her pregnancy with Tarma, keeping the prophet's dark secret locked inside. As time passed, strange natural disasters plagued the land, and the sea grew furiously restless. Jirmak grew too weak to leave her bed, and villagers began pointing fingers at her womb, calling her unborn child an evil seed.

Tarma stopped working to protect her. One rainy morning, seized by sudden labor pains in the mud, villagers ignored her cries. Tarma ran for help on his knees, and his old friend Kurita and his wife rushed out to assist[span_3](start_span)[span_3](end_span).

8

Scene V: The Miracle Birth

Inside the humble hut with its single mat and flickering candle, Jirmak gathered her remaining strength. With a massive push, she gave birth to a gorgeous baby girl amidst blood loss and exhaustion[span_4](start_span)[span_4](end_span).

As the baby was wrapped in a knit blanket, the room turned cold. The baby had glowing skin, blue eyes, and hair like the sea—unlike anyone in the village[span_5](start_span)[span_5](end_span).

9

The Declaration of the Prophecy

Outside, Prophet Kafura arrived proclaiming the future of the child[span_6](start_span)[span_6](end_span). Though villagers called the child a demon and cursed seed, Kafura stood firm, declaring: "She will be a sword in God's hand to bring justice and save a nation. Queen, Empress Rosana.[span_7](start_span)"[span_7](end_span)

When villagers threatened them, Kafura summoned a powerful wind and water tornadoes from the sea to defend Rosana and her family, making the villagers pledge never to harm them[span_8](start_span)[span_8](end_span).

10

The Secret Spell

Wanting a normal life for Rosana, Jirmak asked the prophet for help. Kafura cast a spell: he held Rosana to the sky as white thunder struck the water, turning her blue hair black, her blue eyes brown, and her glowing skin non-glowing[span_9](start_span)[span_9](end_span).

Kafura warned that the spell would break when she turned eight years old, when she dove deep into the sea to save the life of the evil seed of the land[span_10](start_span)[span_10](end_span).

11

Scene IX: Growing Up as a Princess

Moving into the chief leader's house with servants, Rosanna grew up smart and fast-learning. Though raised like a princess, she was forbidden to leave without guards or her mother, sparking a fiery independent spirit[span_11](start_span)[span_11](end_span).

At night, she watched the sea from the rooftop. Every night, Jirmak and Tarma played their pipa and flute, singing the secret lullaby warning of her future journey across the sea[span_12](start_span)[span_12](end_span).

12

The Face of Destiny

On her eighth birthday, defying restrictions under her father's guidance during a village festival, Rosanna stepped outside for the very first time[span_13](start_span)[span_13](end_span).

While Tarma checked a medical prescription at the hospital, Rosanna spotted a boy drowning in the sea. Without hesitation, she jumped in to save him—only to discover he could swim and was just playing[span_14](start_span)[span_14](end_span). That boy was Crown Prince Ian.

13

The Breaking of the Spell

As Rosanna surfaced coughing, her true magical features broke through the prophet's spell: her sea-blue hair, brilliant eyes, and glowing skin returned instantly[span_15](start_span)[span_15](end_span).

Tarma found them, realizing with horror that destiny had claimed her. Back home, Jirmak broke down in tears, blaming Tarma and locking Rosanna away as tension peaked in the palace[span_16](start_span)[span_16](end_span).

14

Scene XII: Journey to the Temple

As the prophecy advanced, Prophet Kafura returned to take Rosanna to the sacred temple mountains for her training, leaving Jirmak heartbroken in tears[span_17](start_span)[span_17](end_span).

Arriving at the 12th chamber—the Servant Chambers—Rosanna faced harsh initiations from Resekelushi, the head priestess's daughter, but quickly defended her dignity and met her loyal friend Elara[span_18](start_span)[span_18](end_span).

15

Scene XIII: The Purple Lily

Demonstrating her royal lineage and exceptional mastery in sewing an ancient purple lily design, Rosanna stunned the head of the Eleventh Chamber and earned an immediate promotion[span_19](start_span)[span_19](end_span).

Prince Ian reappeared, playfully teasing her, giving her a rare necklace, and vowing to return from the capital to make her his queen[span_20](start_span)[span_20](end_span).

16

The Painful Road & Tragedy

Years passed as Rosanna grew into an 18-year-old woman in the temple. Meanwhile, imperial soldiers led a dark mission to capture the blue-haired girl, storming her village, burning houses, and leaving devastation in their wake[span_21](start_span)[span_21](end_span).

Tarma bravely fought the soldiers to protect Rosanna before being brutally struck down. Jirmak rushed to help, striking her head on a jagged rock and losing her sight[span_22](start_span)[span_22](end_span).

17

Arrival in the Capital

Brought to the majestic capital in chains, an outraged Rosanna lunged at King Benson—only to discover that he was none other than Ian, her childhood friend from the lake and temple[span_23](start_span)[span_23](end_span).

King Benson showered her with luxury, but Rosanna burned with hatred for the destruction of her village and family, resisting his affections amidst the fierce jealousy of concubines Tiju and Rika[span_24](start_span)[span_24](end_span).

18

Palace Intrigues & Poison

Concubine Tiju and the Imperial Consort schemed relentlessly against Rosanna, planting a poison cup and cursed doll during a harem gathering to frame her[span_25](start_span)[span_25](end_span).

When the conspiracy collapsed under King Benson's furious investigation, Tiju was executed for burning the slave village, and Rika was banished to the cold palace[span_26](start_span)[span_26](end_span).

19

The Royal Wedding & Covenant

Beneath the moonlight with Prophet Kafura officiating, King Benson and Rosanna exchanged sacred vows, binding their souls as Emperor and Empress of Boparl[span_27](start_span)[span_27](end_span).

Standing side-by-side in golden imperial robes, Queen Rosanna and King Benson purged the corrupt ministers and united their people with compassion and justice[span_28](start_span)[span_28](end_span).

20

Peace and New Life

With the harem dismissed and the corrupt imperial family neutralized, Queen Rosanna discovered she was carrying royal twins[span_29](start_span)[span_29](end_span).

Reunited with King Benson, Prophet Kafura, and her loving mother-in-law, Rosanna found healing and true victory, stepping into her next 25 years of joy and sovereign peace[span_30](start_span)[span_30](end_span).
