The Song of Rosana and the Evil King

Introduction: The Land of Hikmid

Rosana is a village girl born from a handsome sailor called Tarma and a beautiful performer called Jirmak. She was born in a village located in a dry land called Hikmid. Hikmid was two miles far from the sea and had been colonized by a very powerful, rich empire called Boparl. The people of Hikmid were treated like war slaves, yet they were allowed a strange kind of freedom: the Hikmidians were allowed to do whatever they wanted as long as they respected the law.

Boparl consisted of seven states: Kahli, Gorma, Tunera, Farisa, Hjortigar, Hakma, and the capital region. These six states were governed by the king's most trusted ministers, while the king, Shirkisir, his wife Kumata, and his three concubines lived in the capital, Tumar. Tumar was located in the center of the empire, a beautiful city where money flowed, businesses thrived, and gold miners searched for gold. Hikmid was within the state of Farisa to the south of the capital. Rosana's father, Tarma, was a righteous man, the son of a fisherman, Irsha, and a housekeeper named Luisa.

1

Introduction: Jirmak's Past

Rosana's mother, Jirmak, had a different beginning. She was abandoned by her parents at the doorstep of a pleasure house. She was found by Kapoor, the prostitute building owner. Kapoor, charmed by Jirmak's beauty, adopted her and raised her as her daughter. Jirmak was sent to school and trained in the art of dance and music. Kapoor raised Jirmak to carry herself with grace in high social status, harboring a secret hope that Jirmak would marry a rich man and move to a mansion in the capital with servants, enabling Jirmak to provide for Kapoor in her old age.

As Jirmak grew, her beauty became legendary. Men and women from all over the village would gather just to catch a glimpse of her dancing and singing. Jirmak grew more beautiful and elegant day by day. Though many suitors proposed, Kapoor rejected them all, having big plans for her daughter's future.

2

Chapter I: The Rescue and the Vow

One day early in the morning, just as the sun began to break, Jirmak attempted a desperate escape. Driven by the weight of her mother's obsession, she took a small boat to the sea, hoping to find a life of her own. While trying to sail in her little boat, a storm started, and the waves were unforgiving. Jirmak slipped and fell into the sea and she did not know how to swim.

Luckily, Tarma was returning from a long night of fishing with his father, Irsha. Seeing the struggle, Tarma dove deep into the water to save Jirmak. When he brought her back to the sand, Jirmak was not breathing. He tried to revive her, but she remained still. In a final desperate act, Tarma breathed his own air into her lungs. It worked, and she opened her eyes and screamed. Jirmak looked into the eyes of her rescuer, and they both fell deeply in love.

3

Chapter I: Secret Marriage and Exile

Jirmak started meeting Tarma in secret, and their love became deeper and deeper. Two years later, Tarma proposed to Jirmak. Jirmak joyfully accepted, but their happiness was met with a wall of gold and pride. Luisa, Tarma’s mother, could not accept Jirmak's upbringing. She was greatly displeased and banned her son Tarma from seeing Jirmak. Kapoor, Jirmak’s mother, in her greed for wealth, banned Jirmak from seeing Tarma or going outside alone.

Faced with a world that wanted to keep them apart, Tarma and Jirmak chose each other. They married in secret, a quiet vow between their two souls. When the truth came out, they were both cast out by the villagers and disowned by their families. Jirmak's mother found out and decided to disown her; Luisa was displeased by her son's decision and decided to kick him out. With nothing but love and small savings, they built a small hut outside the village.

4

Chapter II: The Birth of Rosana

To provide for their new life, Tarma chose to become a sailor. His days were spent navigating the waves, traveling to the capital to trade merchandise. Jirmak stayed behind on solid ground; her near-drowning experience had left her with a deep fear of the water. Instead, Jirmak leaned into the gifts she was raised with, becoming a renowned dancer and singer. She performed at a famous theater in the neighboring village. The couple found themselves living a successful and stable life. Yet, despite their success, their family still cut all contact with them.

The Meeting of the Prophet

One year later, while Jirmak was gathering supplies in the bustling market, she was approached by a figure of immense presence: a powerful prophet named Kafura.

5

Chapter II: The Prophet's Revelation

Kafura looked deep into Jirmak's eyes and spoke a revelation that chilled her blood.

Prophet Kafura: "You will give birth to the most beautiful woman in the entire world, but greatness comes with a price. Your daughter will face trials of immense pain and challenges that would break a common soul. Yet, she will rise as a powerful queen, the one destined to defeat your people from the grip of the colonizers and change the very course of the world."

Kafura's voice grew urgent.

Prophet Kafura: "Teach her everything you know, Jirmak. Do not hold back your skills, for she will need every ounce of your wisdom to survive the future that awaits her."

Fear gripped Jirmak.

Jirmak: "What mother would wish for her child to suffer? I want my daughter to have a happy, normal life free from pain and life's struggles. Please, prophet, help me! How can I save my daughter from her destiny?"

6

Chapter III: The Cost of the Prophecy

Kafura looked at her with pity and resolve.

Prophet Kafura: "My dear daughter Jirmak, I have been watching you for a very long time; I know the depth of your sacrifice. But even the most powerful prophet cannot rewrite what God has spoken. It is impossible; as Esther, Ruth, and Deborah, she must walk this path. However, I can help you delay the prophecy."

As the prophet turned to leave, his final word lingered in the air like a vow:

Prophet Kafura: "I will return to visit you the moment the promised child is born."

Jirmak returned from the market with the prophet’s words echoing in her mind like a drumbeat. She ran home to her loving husband and told him the good news of her pregnancy, but she kept the fire of that secret locked deep within her, knowing this was no ordinary child.

7

Chapter III: The Stormy Labor

As time passed, Jirmak's physical body grew weak, and her pregnancy became visible to the villagers. The sea became restless, churning with a fury no one had seen before, and strange natural disasters began to plague the land. As months passed, the prophecy began to take a toll on her; Jirmak couldn't walk or leave her bed. The air felt heavy with an omen they couldn't name. The villagers, growing in terror, began to point fingers at Jirmak's growing womb, calling her unborn child an evil seed, which turned into threats to kill both the mother and the unborn child.

Tarma, seeing the danger, made the choice to stop working to care for his family. Because both could not work and had nobody to support them, early in the morning in the cold, Tarma had to go to the sea to fish for food and desperately return before his wife woke up.

8

Chapter IV: Kurita's Help

One gray, raining morning, Jirmak dragged herself outside the hut, her body weak, waiting for her husband's return. Suddenly, a wave of deep labor pains struck her stomach; she collapsed into the mud and screamed in agony. The cruel villagers ignored her and walked away, leaving her alone in the rain. When Tarma finally crossed the hill on his way back, he heard Jirmak crying, dropped his catch, ran to hold her, and realized that she was in labor. Realizing he could not afford any nurse or hospital, Tarma ran to his old friend and begged for help on his knees in the rain. Feeling sorry and remembering old days, his friend had mercy and reached out to help.

Tarma’s friend, Kurita, and his wife did not hesitate. They ran into the freezing rain, lifting Jirmak's limp body.

9

Chapter IV: The Birth Scene

Inside the hut, the reality of their poverty was laid bare: a muddy floor with a single mat and one knit blanket. The only light came from candles flickering against the damp walls. Dishes sat forgotten on the left side of the entry as the room erupted into chaos.

Kurita's wife: "Put her on the floor! Put her on the floor now! Bring me water! I need wipers! Jirmak, breathe, breathe deeply! You've got two lives!"

Jirmak was fading.

Tarma: "Please, please, Jirmak! I need you!"

Jirmak: "It hurts! It hurts! I can't, I can't!"

Tarma: "I need you! I need you, wife! Please, push! Push!"

Kurita's wife: "She is losing too much blood, Tarma! I do not know if she is going to make it!"

Tarma: "She will make it! She has to!"

10

Chapter V: The Miraculous Child

Kurita's wife: "Wifey, you got this! Push!"

With a final gagged breath, Jirmak gathered all her strength and gave one massive, bone-shattering push. The silence that followed was broken by the cry of a baby.

Kurita's wife: "It is a girl! It is a girl!"

She gave birth to a very gorgeous girl.

Tarma: "Thank you, God! We made it!"

But the cost was absolute: the damage from the labor cost Jirmak her health and ability to ever conceive or do hard labor again. As the child was wrapped in the knit blanket and handed to Jirmak and her husband, the room went cold.

Tarma: "What is this? Were you seeing a foreign man behind my back, Jirmak? Everyone in the village looks the same; everyone in the village has dark and brown hair with tan skin and brown eyes. She has blue eyes, hair like the sea, and tan glowing skin."

Villagers: "The prophet is here! Prophet! Prophet! Welcome to our humble village; we are honored to have you as our guest. Please give us your blessing!"

The prophet, his face stern and disappointed, refused to enter their homes:

Prophet Kafura: "No, my children. I am not here to stay, nor am I here to be pleased by your hospitality. I have come on a mission, and I am not allowed to linger. Where is Jirmak?"

The crowd hissed at the name of the woman they had abandoned and tormented:

Villagers: "Jirmak? That prostitute from the pleasure house! We knew you were a righteous man, prophet; give us permission to burn this witch and her demon!"

Chapter IX: The Prophet's Protection

A gasp went through the villagers, but the prophet's voice only grew stronger:

Prophet Kafura: "She will be a sword in God's hand to bring justice and save nations. Queen, Empress Rosana."

Villagers (whispering in fear): "We were right... she is a cursed child. Swords only bring blood."

Tarma (his voice trembling with awe): "My daughter? The promised child? How is that possible?"

Jirmak (clutching the baby to her chest, her eyes wide with terror): "No! No! No! Not my daughter! I won't let them! I will not let her be taken away from me... no, nooo!"

Villagers (sneering toward the hut): "Why, prophet? Why the daughter of a prostitute? We should have killed them earlier and had God choose us!"

Tarma (exploding with a roar of anger): "My wife is not a prostitute!"

He bowed down and carried his wife out of the mud.

Tarma: "Jirmak, you got me! I am here! I will protect our daughter. Nothing will happen to her! I promise."

Chapter XIII: The Spell is Cast

Tarma (with a smile): "It is okay! We are a family. Well, I just wish that my parents were here with me, but it is all good. I will be the father she deserves, and my house is always open for you all."

Jirmak (smiles): "Hopefully, the villagers will be kinder to our daughter."

The villagers looked down in shame and embarrassment.

Prophet Kafura: "I will put a spell on Rosana. I will turn her blue hair into black, her blue eyes into brown, and her glowing skin into non-glowing."

He held Rosana in his arms, lifted her to the sky, and cried out to God. Suddenly, white thunder erupted across the air, striking down until it touched the water with a sharp, echoing sound. As the vibration settled and a single teardrop touched Rosana, her hair changed into black, her blue eyes into brown, and her skin into non-glowing.
